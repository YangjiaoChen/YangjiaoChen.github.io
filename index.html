I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Transportation Systems Engineering at the Georgia Institute of Technology , with a minor in machine learning, under the supervision of Dr. Srinivas Peeta. I earned my M.S. degree in Computational Science and Engineering from Georgia Tech and another M.S. degree in Transportation and Infrastructure Systems Engineering from Purdue University, where I was co-advised by Dr. Samuel Labi and Dr. Srinivas Peeta. Prior to this, I completed my B.E. in Intelligence Transportation Engineering at Beijing Jiaotong University. During my undergraduate studies, I spent one semester as a visiting scholar at the University of Maryland, supervised byDr. Ali Haghani. I was also selected to attend the summer school program jointly organized by Tsinghua University and Technische Universität München in 2016.

My journey in transportation began with a deep interest in connected, automated, and electrified transportation systems, and a desire to contribute to a safer, more intelligent, and environmentally friendly future. My research interests focus on the intersection of security, safety, and human-machine interaction in transportation, specifically encompassing cybersecurity, artificial intelligece, and human factors. My Ph.D. dissertation primarily addresses the evolving cybersecurity challenges associated with connectivity and automation in transportation systems.

In addition to my research, I manage the driving simulator facility at Georgia Tech's Autonomous and Connected Transportation (ACT) Lab, where I oversee experiment planning and equipment maintenance. Previously, I was a research assistant affiliated with the USDOT Center for Connected and Automated Transportation and the Purdue Driving Simulator Lab (DSL). These experiences have equipped me with technical skills in software and hardware development, project management skills, and strong communication abilities.

Outside of my academic pursuits, I am honored to have served as the 2020-2021 President of the Institute of Transportation Engineers (ITE) Georgia Tech Student Chapter and the 2020-2021 Vice President of the American Society of Highway Engineers Student Chapter (ASHE) Georgia Tech Student Chapter. In these roles, I led student activities at Georgia Tech and actively helped students connect with professionals in both academia and industry.
