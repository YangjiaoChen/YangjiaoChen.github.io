    I have a wide range of hobbies and enjoy visiting museums and galleries. Some of my long-standing passions include:


    Dance

    China has 56 officially recognized ethnic groups. I began learning Chinese Ethnic Dance at the age of five, ultimately reaching a semi-professional level (Grade 11). At Beijing Jiaotong University, I was selected to join the Art Troupe, where I participated in numerous performances and competitions. Additionally, I was a member of the Rhythmic Gymnastics Team.


    Calligraphy

    I was introduced to Chinese calligraphy around the same time I started dancing. I won several prizes in Sichuan Province, and practicing calligraphy taught me focus and observation skills. It serves as a calming practice, allowing me to temporarily set aside other concerns.


    Instrument

    I am an amateur guitarist, having learned to play during junior high school and reconnecting with it during snowy days in the Midwest cornfields.

    I'm also passionate about outdoor activities, particularly sports such as swimming, diving, skating, skiing, and skateboarding. Beyond arts and sports, I enjoy traveling and have a keen interest in languages. During my undergraduate studies, I took courses in French, German, Japanese, and Portuguese. Inspired by the Chinese saying, “Read ten thousand books and walk ten thousand miles (讀萬卷書, 行萬里路),” I’m always excited to explore new worlds.
