Driving Behavior & Human-Automation Interaction

  • Human-Automation interaction
    • How to interprete the decision-making process from the psychological and cognitive perspective?
    • How to develop strategies to promote human-AV cooperation?

    During the past years in the Autonomous & Connected Transportation (ACT) Lab at Georgia Tech and the Driving Simulator Lab at Purdue University, I have been actively involved in the design and implementation of various driving simulator experiments. These uniquely designed experiments aim to explore the psychophysiological impacts of real-time information on driver cognition in inherently multitasking congested driving environments, driver takeover behavior under Level 3 vehicular automation, the impacts of the built environment design on the adoption of autonomous vehicles, and the human driver behavior in mixed-flow traffic with connected and autonomous vehicles.
