    Driving Behavior & Human-Automation Interaction

  • Using driving simulator environment to determine interactions between user behavior and infrastructure design under autonomous vehicles
    • How to interprete the decision-making process from the psychological and cognitive perspective?
    • How to develop strategies to promote human-AV cooperation?
  • Impacts of In-vehicle Alert Systems on Situational Awareness and Driving Performance in SAE Level 3 Vehicle Automation
  • Understanding the cognitive and psychological impacts of emerging technologies on driver decision-making using physiological data

    • During the past years in the Autonomous & Connected Transportation (ACT) Lab at Georgia Tech and the Driving Simulator Lab at Purdue University, I have been actively involved in the design and implementation of various driving simulator experiments. These uniquely designed experiments aim to explore the psychophysiological impacts of real-time information on driver cognition in inherently multitasking congested driving environments, driver takeover behavior under Level 3 vehicular automation, the impacts of the built environment design on the adoption of autonomous vehicles, and the human driver behavior in mixed-flow traffic with connected and autonomous vehicles.

    • National Institutes of Health (NIH): Piloting Augmented Reality Cues for Enhancing Driving Safety in Glaucoma, 2022-2023.
    • City of Peachtree Corners, GA: Driving Simulator-Based Study of the Impacts of Various Roadway Design Modifications on the Curiosity Lab Test Track, 2020-2021.
    • U.S. Department of Transportation (USDOT): Using Driving Simulator Environment to Determine Interactions between User Behavior and Infrastructure Design Under Autonomous Vehicles, 2019-202.
    • U.S. Department of Transportation (USDOT): Impacts of In-Vehicle Alert Systems on Situational Awareness and Driving Performance in Level 3 Automation, 2018-2019.
